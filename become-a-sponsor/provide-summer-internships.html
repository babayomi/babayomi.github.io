Introduction to GGIT internship

We have built a smarter way to get your company on board for the summer internship programme

GirlsGoIT (ggit) summer camp programme allows girls between the ages of 16 to 20, from the regions spend their 10 days learning soft skills, Front-end Web development and Data Science, product prototyping using 3D printer and an excursion to visit companies in Chisinau. We have been organising ggit summer camp since 2015.

An important component of the summer camp programme is summer internship, a fantastic way and an opportunity for the camp participants to gain some real working experience for a duration of 1 month (August).

Preparation for internship

Is the first working before the summer camp starts. It is an important process of the internship programme that will ensure ggit team provide quality internship experience for companies and participants ensuring both parties benefit from the internship programme. It starts with expectations drawn to identify standards and skills required by companies in other to provide an internship. These expectations are important and will be added to the learning programme of the 10 days programme.

    1. Fill in the summer internship form and have the possibilities to choose how many interns your company can take
    2. Sign an internship cooperation agreement with TEKEDU (implementing ggit) and assign an internship account manager
    3. Draft a press release on summer camp internship cooperation. Press release will be coordinated with partnered company

Internship selection

Is a process in the summer internship programme whereby participants of ggit summer camp are selected for internship by companies. The selection of interns is done at the closing ceremony of ggit summer camp.

    1. Participants update their cv and submit to the organising team
    2. Organising team put together all the CVs of participants and submit them to companies for reviewing
    3. Companies review CVs and make selections.
    4. Companies review CVs and make selections.
    5. Companies submit the names of the selected interns to GGIT team
    6. Organising team prepares internship award certificate for selected intern
    7. Companies present the internship award to selected Interns at the ggit summer camp closing ceremony
    8. Selected Interns and companies reach a common decision on the start date and days in a week for the internship

Intern On-boarding

Is a process in the summer internship programme whereby selected Interns are ready to start their internship.

    1. Selected intern sign an internship contract with companies (companies can use their contract, otherwise the organising team can provide an internship contract)
    2. Company organise orientation session for selected intern
