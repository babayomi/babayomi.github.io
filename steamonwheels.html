Problem Statement

Despite Moldova becoming a hotspot for IT outsourcing with a fivefold increase in IT product and service exports over the past five years, women remain underrepresented in the IT sector

Design goal:

A robust outreach program aimed at raising awareness among girls and boys aged 13 to 20 about the opportunities in STEAM education and careers.

Expected results:

  • More than 50,000 young people reached through national level implemented campaigns
  • Deliver 1,000 STEAM workshops nationwide including Refugee centres Triangulation of competitive benchmarking and user-centred design workshop

Research and concept validation

I researched international outreach programs that adopt innovative approaches to STEAM education for young people living in rural communities.

During my studies, I came across Code Mobile, a fully equipped coding lab-on-wheels that serves as a comprehensive STEAM outreach program for Canadian teens, capable of driving to various locations and organising workshops.

I spoke with Canada Learning Code, the administrator of the Code Mobile outreach program, where I learned about the Canadian organisation's inspiration behind starting Code Mobile and her commitment to:

  • Serving low-income communities
  • Committed to reach every corner in Canada
  • Indoor/outdoor learning, STEAM hands-on activities

After engaging with Code Mobile and Canada Learning Code, I noticed many similarities in our outreach plans and commitments. This led me to reflect on the groundbreaking initiative ‚ÄúSTEAMonWheels‚Äù.

I started putting together a technical proposal encompassing a project description, outcomes, outputs and planned activities, budget, and a dedicated monitoring and evaluation framework.

Visual Design

The process of creating the STEAM on Wheel brand required a strategic and creative approach to establish a unique and memorable identity. Through research on existing STEAM trends, it became evident that successful brands incorporate vibrant colours, STEAM field icons, and geometric shapes in their visual designs.

Icons

In my initial design step, I conducted online research to find STEAM icons. I discovered a variety of them on IconScout and downloaded several options to choose the most suitable ones for STEAM on Wheels. Opting for a flat icon style, as it aligns better with the brand's identity.

Logo

My next design task is to create an eye-catching logo that represents STEAM on Wheels and incorporates STEAM elements. It will be wrapped on the bus to attract attention. I considered the following key features before designing the logo:

  • Appeal to young people
  • Reflect the essence of STEAM
  • Incorporate a representation of a vehicle or moving wheels

After contemplating the logo's essential elements, I opted for a text-based approach for the STEAM on Wheels logo, which will be transformed into a vector object.

Once the text was converted into a vector object, I made the decision to apply colours to the text-based logo.

How can I add STEAM elements/icons to the text-based logo? After experimenting with a few examples, I concluded that embedding STEAM elements within the text-based logo was the best approach.

The last step on logo design is how can I make the text-based logo with embedded elements show it is on wheels and has the GirlsGoIT logo represented as her initiative? I experimented with few examples and concluded on this

The official font chosen for STEAM on Wheels is Inter. This font was selected for its numerous stylistic variations, aiding in scaling the optical size and weight, as well as its support for multiple languages.

Communication design

Design thinking

Paula Popescu, project manager STEAMonWheels at communication design thinking workshop

To achieve the expected result, I organised a design thinking workshop with the project team recruited for STEAMonWheels. The workshop is to design audience experience and their interaction with STEAMonWheels.

We agreed on 3 key questions for the workshop:

  • How should the audience learn about STEAMonWheels?
  • How do our audience interact with STEAMonWheels?
  • What happens after the audience interacts with STEAMonWheels?

We created an ‚Äúaudience persona‚Äù to profile who are STEAMonWheels ideal audience:

  • Interested in STEAM and IT education but I have nowhere to learn in my community
  • Ukrainian girls and boys living in refugee centres across Moldova
  • Have no previous experience or have not interacted with STEAM or IT
  • Living in villages/remote areas with no infrastructure for STEAM or IT
  • What happens after I have interacted with SpR? What happens next?

We learn alot during workshop, these are some of our high level findings:

Paula Popescu, project manager STEAMonWheels at communication design thinking workshop

How should the audience learn about STEAMonWheels:

  • Launch event and communication materials to promote STEAMonWheels online and offline
  • Travel journey map to establish travel destinations and timeline
  • STEAMonWheels collaboration with media, youth centres at local level

How do our audience interact with STEAMonWheels:

  • Team building and fun activities organised during STEAM workshops
  • Branded STEAMonWheels souvenirs for audience participating at STEAMonWheels workshop
  • Development of STEAM curricular
  • Short video documentary per workshop of how audience are engaged in STEAMonWheels workshop
  • Testimonial video of at least 2 participants per workshop sharing STEAMonWheels experience

What happens after audience interacts with STEAMonWheels

  • Printed materials about education opportunities offered by technical university and Vocational education with STEAM and IT faculties
  • Audience feedback form
  • Local media invited to STEAMonWheels workshop

Overall conclusion on STEAMonWheels design thinking workshop

  • Design a website for the audience to request for STEAMonWheels in their village or community. The website will consist of home page, about us, media kit and contact us
  • Use the existing network of GirlsGoIT to create STEAMonWheels travel journey map. GirlsGoIT a local club network in 33 regions in Moldova
  • Promote STEAMonWheels at the national level targeting youth, parents, teachers using Google Ads
  • Organise official STEAMonWheels launch event with donors
  • Design and print souvenirs e.g. tshirts, bags, pen, notebooks

Results of STEAMonWheel activities

  • More than 4,000 young people reached the STEAM workshop. 60% are girls
  • Over 400 workshop organised. 42 of which were held in refugee centres
  • Over 87% of workshops took place in the rural regions
  • Approximately 64% (2,562 teenagers) of respondents were highly motivated to pursue new studies in the field of STEAM as a result of participating in the STEAMonWheels workshop
