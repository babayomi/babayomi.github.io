Tarom

Tarom home page Interaction Design sketched using pen and paper

Problem Statement

This case study is the outcome of my Professional UX Design Course at UX Design Institute. To design a new desktop website for Tarom focusing on the flight booking process: how users search for flights, booking and payment.

Tarom is the flag carrier and oldest currently operating airline of Romania, based in Otopeni near Bucharest.

There are flaws in Tarom desktop website flight booking, several unnecessary information and pain point.

Design goal:

Create an online experience that is fast, easy, and intuitive: one that’s based on understanding of the target users.

Design steps:

  • Research: Competitve benchmarking and note taking of two usability tests
  • Analysis: Affinity diagram, customer journey map
  • Design: Flow diagram, interaction design, mid-fidelity prototype
  • Handover: Annoted wireframe

Role:

UX Research, UX Design

Duration:

6 months (September 2021 to February 2022)

Tools used:

  • Pen and paper
  • Google docs and slides
  • Miro
  • Figma
  • Adobe Illustrator

Research

Competitive Benchmarking

I started this project with the analysis of Tarom and Air Moldova to get myself acquainted with their standards, best practices and drawbacks. I choose these airlines because they are both Romanian airline, they offer similar services.

The competitive research has provided me with insights regarding what to avoid when designing a booking flow, as well as inspiration and guidance to follow best practices.

Usability Testing

In the discovery phase of this project, i conducted user testing and interviews in order to get a better understanding of user problems. I asked users to book flights on Tarom and Air Moldova desktop web applications, recorded their screens and videoed and analysed their interactions whilst asking them to talk me through their decisions to determine their pain points, app conventions and behaviours.

Note Taking

I learnt the value of conducting usability tests in research by creating a bank of insights that will be used during the future stages of this project. I watch the recordings of 2 users usability tests provided.

Printscreen from note taking

Conclusion: User 1 & 2 suggest both airline should improve their interface design and let user type their date of birth and nationality rather than scrolling.

Home page should focus primarily on booking and managing flights, any other information should be displayed at the end of the booking process.

Analysis

Affinity Diagram

Now that I have all this research data from multiple sources, I needed a way to converge and make sense of it all. I decided on an Affinity Diagram as it helps structure research data, uncover patterns, and most importantly, prioritize and focus my attention on what could actually be making a difference in the user experience of booking a flight.

The process: going through the research, gather concise observations and uncover patterns from all three types of research, stick them all to one place for full visibility, start organizing post-it notes into the following groups.

The affinity diagram also provided valuable data about the users' goals, pain points, mental models, behavior and good practices that users applaud and look for, which were not necessarily related to the booking process, so I grouped them separately.

View Affinity Diagram document

Customer Journey Map

Going further, I wanted to envision how users interact with the flight booking process and to organize all data in a structured matter.

To do that, I elaborated a Customer Journey Map, based on the Affinity Diagram and included the different goals, behaviors, pain points and mental models that a user has while booking a flight.

The Customer Journey Map was an important step of this project as I have referred back to it many times in my design decision-making process.

View Customer Journey Map

Design

Flow Diagram

After an in-depth review of the Customer Journey Map, I set out to define a high-level flow for booking flights that would address the issues highlighted by my research.

The Flow Diagram illustrates the steps a user takes to search and choose flights, insert passenger information, extra options and payment.

View Flow Diagram

Interaction Design

Starting from the Flow Diagram, I went on to define the main interactions the user will encounter on the website. I have taken into consideration and carefully crafted the experience based on the research results and the best practices discovered in the competitive benchmarking, as well as on the constant testing of the flows and interaction with friends who were amongst the target users.
